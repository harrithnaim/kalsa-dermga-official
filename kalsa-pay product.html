What is Kalsa Pay

A closed-loop e-wallet built for schools

Money loaded by a parent can only be spent at that child’s own school — at the canteen or the bookstore. The wristband is just a key: it holds no money and no personal data, so a lost band is useless the moment a parent freezes it in the app.

📶

Tap to pay

The merchant’s own Android phone becomes the terminal. The child taps their wristband; payment clears in about a second.

👛

Parents in control

Top up the wallet, set a daily and per-meal limit, and freeze a lost wristband instantly — all from the app.

🧾

Automatic bookkeeping

Every sale is recorded. The canteen and bookstore see live daily takings instead of counting cash by hand.

How it works

From top-up to tap in four steps

1

Parent tops up

The parent adds credit to their child’s wallet from the Kalsa Pay app.

2

Child taps

At the canteen or bookstore, the child taps their NFC wristband on the merchant’s phone.

3

Instant check

The system checks the balance and the parent’s limits, then approves in about a second.

4

Everyone sees it

The parent sees the purchase in the app; the merchant sees it in their daily sales.

Where it works

One wristband, every counter in the school

The same wristband and the same wallet work across every Kalsa Pay outlet in the school. Adding a new counter is just installing the app and signing in — no new hardware.

🍜 Canteen

Meals and drinks, fast queues at lunch, no coins to lose, no change to count.

📚 Bookstore

Exercise books, stationery and uniforms — the same tap, the same parent oversight.

➕ More to come

Any school outlet can be added: co-op, events, printing. One balance covers them all.

Safety & privacy

Built carefully, because it’s children and money

🔒

The band holds nothing

No money and no personal data on the wristband — only a serial. Frozen instantly if lost.

🛡️

Limits enforced

Spending limits and balances are checked on the server on every tap, and can never be overspent.

🤝

Money stays transparent

Parents pay the school committee directly; every ringgit is recorded and reconciled.
