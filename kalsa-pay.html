How It Works

Three steps, no cash

A wristband, a phone, and a canteen tablet. Nothing else to install.

Step One

Parent tops up

Money is added to the child's wallet from the parent app. During the pilot, parents pay the school committee directly by DuitNow and the balance is credited the same day.

Step Two

Child taps to pay

The student taps their wristband on the canteen tablet. The balance is checked and the purchase recorded instantly — no PIN for a child to forget, no notes to lose, nothing to leave in a bag.

Step Three

Everyone sees it

The purchase appears in the parent app immediately, and in the canteen operator's daily sales total. Nobody counts cash at the end of the day.

For Parents

Control that sits in your pocket

Freeze a wristband instantly

Wristband lost or damaged? Deactivate it yourself from the app. No phone call, no waiting for an administrator.

Set a daily limit

Cap how much your child can spend in a day. The limit is enforced at the moment of the tap, not after the fact.

See every purchase

An itemised history of what was bought and when — useful for allergies, budgets, and awkward conversations about ice cream.

Worn, not carried

A wristband stays on the child. There is no card to leave in a desk, drop in the playground, or forget at home — the most common reason cashless school schemes fail on day one.

Balance can never go negative

There is no overdraft and no debt. If the wallet is empty, the tap is simply declined.

For Canteen Operators

Your takings, counted for you

Reporting

Automatic daily totals

Gross sales, the 1% platform fee, and your net figure — calculated from the transaction record for any day or date range. No tallying at closing time.

Corrections

One-tap refunds

Charged the wrong item? Reverse the transaction from the same tablet and the money returns to the student's wallet immediately.

Reliability

No double charges

Every tap carries a unique key, so a dropped connection and a retry can never charge a student twice for the same nasi lemak.

Children's Data

What we deliberately do not collect

This system is used by children, so the honest answer about data matters more than the feature list.

We do not use

— Facial recognition of any kind
— Biometrics or fingerprints
— IC numbers
— Advertising, tracking or third-party analytics
— Location tracking of students

How the rest is handled

— Wristband IDs are stored as one-way hashes, never in raw form
— The optional tap snapshot is a manual-review photo only, auto-deleted after 48 hours
— Parental consent is collected in writing before a child is enrolled, per the PDPA
— Staff see only the records their role requires
— Records are deleted 90 days after a student leaves

Top-up payments are processed by a licensed payment provider; Kalsa Pay never stores your bank card details. Full detail is set out in our privacy policy .

Current Status

Running as a school pilot

Kalsa Pay is in pilot with a small number of Malaysian schools while we prove the day-to-day operation with real canteens and real families. Onboarding takes about five minutes per family at a parents' meet-up.

During the pilot, parents pay the school committee directly and the wallet acts as the record of that balance. Kalsa Dermaga does not hold parents' money.
